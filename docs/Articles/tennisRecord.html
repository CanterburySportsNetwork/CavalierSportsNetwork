Canterbury Boys Tennis Season Recap



The Canterbury boys tennis team had a record of 8-11, with many close matches against great teams and a very difficult schedule. They played and competed with 2 current state finalists, Homestead and Culver Academy throughout the regular season. They lost to eventual state finalists, Homestead, in sectionals.

Records:

Roshen Kulkarni: 1 singles, 12-7,
Christian Urberg: 2 singles, 1, 2 doubles, 12-7
Rohan Jain: 2, 3 singles 1, 2 doubles, 12-6
Avinash Singh: 1 doubles, 4-14
Preston Reeves: 1, 2 doubles, 4-14
Saif Ibrahim: 3 singles, 1, 2 doubles, 2-15
Arjun Yaram: 1, 2 doubles, 4-15
Neil Boyd: 3 singles, 2 doubles, 3-1
Riley Mitchell: 2 doubles, 1-0

Captains: Roshen Kulkarni, Arjun Yaram

Roshens favorite memory this season: My favorite memory was the Carroll Invitational because we were able to play talented teams from around the state, and we did well and came together as a team

How would you assess the season? The team did well overall as most of the team was stepping into new positions. We had many close matches that we were unable win that we should have, but overall I am happy with the season overall. Everyone improved as the season went on and gave 100 percent effort. Despite not winning sectionals, we played well against a very good team in homestead.

Other than school and tennis, Roshen enjoys playing the Cello in the Canterbury Orchestra, Lifting weights, and playing golf. He is very involved in the Canterbury community as he is the Founder and Leader of Plant Club, a member of DECA club, CSN, and a writer for the Untitled newspaper.
